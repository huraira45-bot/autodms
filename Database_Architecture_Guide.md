# Enterprise Database Architecture & Integration Guide

This document serves as a high-level manual for developers, analysts, and project managers on how to navigate the 433 tables within the `db1` ecosystem. 

The database is heavily structured and module-based. Below is a breakdown of the different aspects of the system, their core tables, and the expected lifecycle of data within them.

---

## 1. General Master Data & Configuration (`gen_` prefix)
Before any transactions occur in the system, master configurations must exist. These tables form the backbone for dropdowns, lookups, and system logic.

* **Core Tables:**
  * `gen_PartiesInfo`: The central registry for all human/company interactions. Customers, suppliers, vendors, and contractors are all treated as "Parties".
  * `gen_EmployeeInfo`: Registry for all internal staff, techs, and management.
  * `gen_BranchInfo` & `gen_CompanyInfo`: Used to segment data for multi-location or multi-company ERP rollouts.
* **How to use it:** Whenever you need the name, address, or phone number of an entity linked to an invoice or job card, you will join on `PartyID` linking back to `gen_PartiesInfo`.

---

## 2. Automotive Service & Workshop Flow (`addata_` prefix)
This module handles the lifecycle of a vehicle entering a dealership or workshop, undergoing repair, and exiting post-payment.

* **Core Tables:**
  * `addata_CustomerInfo`: Tracks the Vehicle (Chassis / Engine No.) and links it to a Party/Owner.
  * `Addata_JobCardInfo`: The primary transaction table. It acts as the "ticket" for the car in the shop.
  * `addata_CustomerInvoiceInfo`: The financial footprint of the completed Job Card.
  * `addata_CustomerInvoiceRecovery`: Logs the actual cash/bank receipt settling the invoice.
* **Lifecycle / Dataflow:**
  1. **Intake:** Car arrives → Record is pulled/created in `addata_CustomerInfo`.
  2. **Service:** `Addata_JobCardInfo` is generated. Parts are added to `Addata_JobCardInfoPartsDetail` and labor assigned to tables like `...subjobDetail`.
  3. **Billing:** Once `JobStatus` is complete, an `addata_CustomerInvoiceInfo` is cut. The system calculates Tax, Parts Total, and Insurance Salvage here.
  4. **Payment:** Customer pays via cash/card → `addata_CustomerInvoiceRecovery` logs the receipt to close out the receivable.

---

## 3. Inventory & Procurement Management
Handling the physical flow of goods, from order to warehouse, to consumption.

* **Core Tables:**
  * `InventItems`: The master catalog for all physical goods (spare parts, oil, vehicles).
  * `InventWareHouse`: Locations where items live.
  * `data_PurchaseOrder` & `data_StockArrivalInfo`: Tracks inbound procurement.
  * `data_StockInOutInfo` / `addata_StockIssuancetoPosKitchen`: Translates to goods entering or leaving the warehouse.
* **Lifecycle / Dataflow:**
  1. Master items are created in `InventItems`.
  2. The procurement team issues a `data_PurchaseOrder`.
  3. Goods physically arrive and the gate generates a `data_InwardGatePassInfo`.
  4. The inventory balance climbs when logged into `data_StockArrivalInfo`.
  5. The workshop consumes parts (`addata_StockIssuetoJobCard`), drastically deducting from available warehouse stock.

---

## 4. Sales & Retail Operations
Tracking direct over-the-counter sales, POS transactions, and tracker system assignments.

* **Core Tables:**
  * `addata_SaleInfo` & `addata_SaleDetail`: Basic header-detail hierarchy for sales orders.
  * `addata_TrackerAssigmentinfo`: A specialized module used to track serial numbers/tracking IDs linked to specific vehicles and clients.
* **How to use it:** To build a retail sales dashboard, you would query `addata_SaleInfo` and aggregate the `NetAmount` grouped by `SaleDate` and filtered by `BranchID`.

---

## 5. Financial & General Ledger System (`GL` prefix)
The final destination for all financial consequences generated by other modules. Whether a part is sold or an employee is paid, it writes to the Ledger.

* **Core Tables:**
  * `GLChartOFAccount`: The complete tree of the company's asset, liability, equity, revenue, and expense accounts.
  * `GLvMAIN`: The Master "Voucher". Represents a single accounting event (e.g., Bank Receipt, Journal Entry).
  * `GLvDetail`: The debit and credit lines of the voucher. Must always balance out to zero.
  * `GLFiscalYear` & `GLPeriods`: Financial boundaries defining when books are open or closed.
* **Lifecycle / Dataflow:**
  1. A transaction occurs in the Automotive module (e.g., a Job Card Invoice).
  2. The system automatically triggers a background entry creating a `GLvMAIN` header.
  3. `GLvDetail` lines are written: *Debiting Accounts Receivable (The Customer)* and *Crediting Sales Revenue*.
  4. Financial controllers view the `GLBalanceSheetHead` to ensure accounting integrity.

---

## Best Practices for Custom Development

1. **Multi-Tenancy Filtering:** Most transactional tables contain a `CompanyID`, `BranchID`, and `FiscalID`. *Always* filter by these columns in your SQL queries to ensure financial data isn't bleeding across subsidiaries or closed financial years.
2. **Audit Trails:** Note the `EntryUserID`, `EntryUserDateTime`, and `ModifyUserID` heavily present across tables. Do not strip these when inserting new data manually or building new web apps; they are crucial for security audits.
3. **Soft Deletes vs. Hard Deletes:** Before issuing a `DELETE` command, check if the table utilizes a `Status` or `IsActive` bit flag. Enterprise architectures often prefer soft deletes.
